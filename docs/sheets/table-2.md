The long-form (Janet) layout is the **natural** shape of the periodic table: it places the s-, f-, d-, and p-blocks side by side in strict Aufbau order, with no block detached. Every column directly maps to one step in the quantum shell-filling sequence, making the underlying structure immediately visible. The familiar [18-column layout](/sheets/table-1) is a practical abbreviation that removes the f-block from the main body to fit on a standard page — at the cost of breaking the true left-to-right ordering. See [§ 6.7 — The Periodic Table Emerges](/chapters/6-spin#67-the-periodic-table-emerges) for the full quantum-mechanical derivation of both layouts.
